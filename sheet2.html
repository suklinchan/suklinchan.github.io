What Bad Driving Behaviors Contribute To Fatal Collisions?

It'd be interesting to find out how the different types of negligent driving behaviors correlate with the total number of collisions. We want to answer questions like: Which behavior could be a good predictor of a driver getting involved in crashes again? For any state with similiar number of total crashes, which one has the worst drivers in terms of a particular irresponsible behavior?

Use the dropdown menu to select any of the driving behaviors, and see how they relate to the total number of collisions. You might find that of all of the behaviors, alcohol impairment has a relatively stronger linear relationship with the total number of collisions. Distracted driving appears to be consistently below 5 across most states, with Mississippi and Wisconsin being the outliers. For the states with the highest number of total collisions (more than 23 per billion miles): South Carolina, West Virginia and North Dakota, South Carolina has the worst drivers in terms of speeding. Have fun exploring!
