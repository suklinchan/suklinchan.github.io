Where Are the Worst Drivers in US

To understand which state has the worst drivers, we need to explore multiple factors and the relationship between some of them. The number of collisions alone is not a sufficient indicator of the prevalence of bad driving. We need to factor in the total miles traveled in each state and focus on the characteristics of the drivers who were involved in fatal collisions. The cost of auto insurance could also indicate quality of driving in a state.

In this narrative visualization, we will examine multiple measures which could be indicators of bad driving: number of car crashes in each state, and out of these, the car crashes associated with bad driving behaviors: speeding, alcohol impairment, distractions, and involvement in previous accidents, how much insurance companies pay out, and how much insurance companies charge drivers. We will use data from the year 2012 for this exercise. The conclusion could differ with data for a different year.
