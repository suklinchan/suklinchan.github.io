Worst Drivers by States

Which state has the worst drivers? The answer is much more complicated than you thought. The number of car crashes, even fatal ones, isn’t a clear-cut way to understand who is and who isn’t a bad driver. A state with more fatal car crashes is not necessarily a state with more bad drivers — it could simply be a state that has more drivers or a state with worse driving conditions. So we need to factor in the total miles traveled in each state and focus on the characteristics of the drivers who were involved in fatal collisions.

In this narrative visualization, we will try to find out "which state has the worst drivers" using multiple measures which could be indicators of bad driving: number of car crashes in each state, and out of these, the car crashes associated with bad driving behaviors: speeding, alcohol impairment, distractions, and involvement in previous accidents, how much insurance companies pay out, and how much insurance companies charge drivers. We will use data from the year 2012 for this exercise. The conclusion could differ with data for a different year.
