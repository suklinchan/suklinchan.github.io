How Much Does Bad Driving Cost?

Average premiums in each state could reflect insurance companiesâ€™ overall assessment of who is likely to cost them in the future. According to the figures from the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) in 2012, high-risk drivers are to be found in New Jersey, where at $1,302, car insurance is the most expensive in the country. On that same logic, Idahoans, whose car insurance is on average less than half that, are the best drivers in the country. But how much do irresponsible driving behaviors per se influence the cost of insurance?

Use the dropdown menu to select any of the driving behaviors as well as the total number of crashes, and see how they relate to the insurance premium across the country. You might not be able to find a direct correlation in all cases. In reality, there are many other factors which come into play. For example, the model of the car, how much the drivers drive, and other demographic considerations such as where the drivers reside, age and driving experience. It is interesting to see that inspite of a low overall number of crashes and relatively well behaved driving population, the premium at $1,274 is super high, just second to the highest at New Jersey. South Carolina, with a relatively high number of crashes and bad drivers, is not particularly penalized with a high premium. Check it out yourself!
