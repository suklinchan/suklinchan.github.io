According to the figures from the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) in 2012, high-risk drivers are to be found in New Jersey, where at $1,302, car insurance is the most expensive in the country. By the same logic, Idaho drivers, whose car insurance is on average less than half that, would be considered the best drivers in the country. But does the data corroborate with this logic?

It is interesting to see that inspite of a low overall number of crashes and relatively well behaved driving population, the premium at $1,274 is super high at District of Columbia, just second to the highest at New Jersey. Can you find the state(s) which have high number of crashes and bad drivers, but are not penalized with a high premium? What conculsion, if any, can you infer from the data?

In reality, there are many other factors which come into play. For example, the model of the car, how much the drivers drive, and other demographic considerations such as where the drivers reside, age and driving experience.
