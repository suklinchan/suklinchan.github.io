Which State Has The Worst Drivers?

A state with more fatal car crashes is not necessarily a state with more bad drivers â€” it could simply be a state that has more drivers or a state with worse driving conditions. We need to factor in the total miles traveled in each state and focus on the characteristics of the drivers who were involved in fatal collisions. In this and subsequent charts, we always refer to the number of drivers involved in fatal collisions per billion miles.

Use the dropdown menu to view the number of drivers who were speeding, driving under the influence of alcohol, distracted, or have been involved in previous accidents. These are stacked against the total number of fatal collisions to provide a visual perspective of how prevalent is each negligent behavior out of the total in each state. Note that the negligent behaviors are not mutually exclusive: a driver could be both distracted and speeding prior to the collision.
